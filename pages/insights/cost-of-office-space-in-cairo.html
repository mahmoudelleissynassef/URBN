How to read these numbers

Office costs in Cairo are harder to compare than in many markets. Rents are usually quoted in Egyptian pounds (EGP), increasingly in USD for prime stock, and fit-out condition ranges from a bare shell to a fully finished suite. That means two buildings advertising a similar "rent" can carry very different total costs once service charges, fit-out and parking are added.

The figures in this guide are indicative ranges intended for early budgeting — not formal quotes. They move with district, building grade, floor, lease term and the EGP/USD position at the time you sign. What drives office cost in Cairo

  • District. Prime submarkets such as New Cairo & the Fifth Settlement and the Nile-front CBD command the highest rents; Sheikh Zayed, Maadi, Heliopolis and Nasr City typically offer better value.
  • Building grade. Modern Grade A towers with reliable power, parking and amenities price well above older or secondary stock.
  • Fit-out condition. A shell-and-core floor carries a large one-off fit-out cost; a fitted or serviced suite bundles that into the rent or fee.
  • Currency — EGP vs USD. Prime landlords increasingly quote or index rents in USD, which shifts your cost as the exchange rate moves. Clarify the currency and any indexation up front.
  • Lease term & flexibility. Longer commitments can secure better rates and incentives; short, flexible terms (serviced and coworking) cost more per desk but reduce risk and CAPEX.

Service charge, fit-out & CAPEX

On a conventional lease, the headline rent is rarely your full cost. Plan for two further lines:

  • Service charge. A separate per-square-metre-per-month charge covering common-area maintenance, security and shared building services. Confirm what it includes and whether utilities are separate.
  • Fit-out (CAPEX). A one-off capital cost to take a shell or category-A floor to a usable, branded workspace — partitions, meeting rooms, IT, finishes and furniture. This varies widely with specification and can be a meaningful share of first-year cost.

A serviced or coworking option folds most of these into a single monthly fee, trading a higher recurring cost for little or no upfront CAPEX — often the better route for teams that value speed and flexibility.

Coworking & serviced offices: per-desk pricing

Flexible space in Cairo is priced per desk per month, with the figure depending on the format and location. As an indicative ordering — lowest to highest cost per person:

  • Hot desk — an unassigned seat in a shared area; the most affordable option, suited to individuals and part-time use.
  • Dedicated desk — your own fixed desk in a shared space; a step up in cost for permanence and storage.
  • Private serviced suite — a lockable, fully fitted office for your team, priced per desk but at the top of the range, with reception, meeting rooms and IT included.

Because per-desk pricing bundles rent, service charge, fit-out, utilities and amenities, it is the simplest number to compare across providers — but read the inclusions carefully (meeting-room credits, after-hours access, parking) as they differ. How to budget for a Cairo office

  1. Size the requirement. Estimate desks, then translate to area (allowing for meeting rooms and circulation) if you are considering conventional space.
  2. Build an all-in figure. For a lease: rent + service charge + estimated fit-out (amortised) + utilities + parking. For flexible space: the per-desk fee plus any extras.
  3. Account for currency. If rent is in or indexed to USD, stress-test your budget against exchange-rate movement.
  4. Compare routes. Put a conventional lease and a serviced/coworking option side by side on the same all-in, like-for-like basis before deciding.

Cost of office space in Cairo — FAQs

How much does office space cost in Cairo in 2026?

As an indicative guide, prime Grade A asking rents commonly sit around EGP 350–700 / m² / month in leading districts like New Cairo and the Nile CBD, with serviced offices and coworking priced per desk. These are rough ranges only — actual cost depends on district, grade, fit-out, floor, term and whether rent is quoted in EGP or USD. We confirm live pricing at source per option.

What is included in the rent versus the service charge?

Headline rent usually covers the leased area only. A separate service charge covers common-area maintenance, security and shared services. Conventional leases may also leave fit-out, utilities and parking as extra costs, while serviced and coworking offices bundle most of these into one per-desk or per-suite fee. Always compare on an all-in basis.

How much should I budget for fit-out?

Fit-out (CAPEX) varies widely with specification — a basic fit can be modest, a full Grade A fit-out is a significant one-off cost. Budget it as a separate capital line on top of rent, or choose a serviced/coworking option where fit-out is already built into the monthly fee.
