The Accra office market

Accra is Ghana's capital and one of West Africa's most stable, business-friendly hubs. As an English-speaking gateway to the region, it draws demand from oil & gas, finance, technology firms and development organisations, supported by a deep pool of multinationals and regional offices. Grade A development is concentrated in Airport City and the Ridge corporate corridor, while established residential-corporate districts such as Cantonments, Osu, Labone and East Legon offer a mix of purpose-built and converted office space. For occupiers, that means a genuine choice between modern towers, fitted floors, serviced suites and flexible coworking.

Because prime rents are frequently quoted in US dollars while secondary stock is priced in Ghana cedis (GHS), and because fit-out condition varies widely, comparing options on a like-for-like basis is hard. Prime office districts in Accra

Where to base your team depends on commute, client proximity and budget. The main clusters:

Airport City

The dominant Grade A submarket — modern towers and business parks near Kotoka International Airport, with the highest concentration of multinational HQs and serviced offices.

Ridge

Central corporate corridor with established towers and prestige addresses, favoured by finance, legal and government-facing firms.

Cantonments

Leafy diplomatic district popular with embassies, NGOs and energy firms, with a mix of converted residences and purpose-built offices.

Osu

Vibrant, well-connected commercial district close to the coast, popular with creative, tech and SME teams seeking a central base.

Labone

Quiet, upmarket residential-corporate pocket between Cantonments and Osu, with boutique offices and managed suites.

East Legon

Fast-growing residential-corporate district to the north-east, with newer Grade A space, coworking and good value for growing teams.
