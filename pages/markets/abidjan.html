The Abidjan office market

Abidjan is the economic capital of Côte d'Ivoire and a leading business hub of Francophone West Africa. As the heart of the UEMOA zone — home to the BCEAO central bank and the regional stock exchange, the BRVM — the city anchors banking, telecoms and multinational regional offices across the sub-region. Strong growth has drawn corporate headquarters and regional teams to prestige addresses on the Plateau and to upscale districts like Cocody, while business and expat-oriented clusters in Marcory and Zone 4 continue to expand. For occupiers, that means a genuine choice between modern towers, fitted floors, serviced suites and flexible coworking.

Because rents are usually quoted in CFA francs (XOF) and increasingly in USD for prime stock, and because fit-out condition varies widely, comparing options on a like-for-like basis is hard. URBN does that work for you — confirming what is actually available, at what price, and on what terms.

Prime office districts in Abidjan

Where to base your team depends on commute, client proximity and budget. The main clusters:

Plateau

The CBD and prime business district — modern towers, banks and the highest concentration of multinational HQs and serviced offices.

Cocody

Upscale residential and embassy district, favoured for prestige addresses, professional services and regional headquarters.

Marcory & Zone 4

Business and expat hub with a growing mix of office space, popular with tech, energy and trading firms looking for value.

Riviera

Fast-growing eastern district with newer stock and good access for teams living across Cocody and the eastern suburbs.

Treichville

Central, well-connected district near the port and Plateau — practical mid-market office space for commercial and back-office teams.

Greater Abidjan

Emerging corridors beyond the core districts, with new-build stock coming to market as the city continues to expand.
