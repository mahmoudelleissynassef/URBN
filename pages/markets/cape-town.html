The Cape Town office market

Cape Town is one of South Africa's leading business cities and the country's technology, BPO/contact-centre and creative hub — the heart of the "Silicon Cape" ecosystem. Alongside a strong financial-services base and a major tourism economy, the city has become a magnet for remote-work teams and satellite offices, pushing demand for both polished Grade A space and flexible workspace. For occupiers, that means a genuine choice between modern CBD and Waterfront towers, mixed-use precincts in Century City, established Southern Suburbs addresses, fitted floors, serviced suites and coworking.

Because rents are usually quoted in South African rand (ZAR) on a gross basis, and because fit-out condition and building grade vary widely between nodes, comparing options on a like-for-like basis is hard. Prime office districts in Cape Town

Where to base your team depends on commute, client proximity and budget. The main nodes:

CBD & Foreshore

The central business core — corporate towers, financial and professional-services firms, and a dense cluster of serviced offices and coworking near the city centre.

V&A Waterfront

Cape Town's prime address — premium waterfront offices favoured by headquarters, finance and tech firms wanting a flagship presence.

Century City

Master-planned mixed-use precinct with modern Grade A stock, good parking and easy highway access — popular with corporates and BPO operators.

Claremont & Southern Suburbs

Established commercial node serving the leafy southern suburbs, with a mix of corporate offices, professional firms and growing flexible space.

Bellville & Tyger Valley

Northern-suburbs business hub with good value and access for teams living in the north, anchored by finance, insurance and back-office operations.

Woodstock & the City Fringe

Creative and tech-led fringe district of converted warehouses and lofts, popular with startups, agencies and the "Silicon Cape" community.

Types of office space in Cape Town

Grade A & whole-floor

Full floors and HQ space in modern towers for established teams that want their own identity and long-term certainty.

Serviced & managed offices

Private, fully fitted suites with reception, meeting rooms and IT included — move-in ready on flexible terms.

Coworking & flexible desks

Hot desks and dedicated desks for startups, project teams and satellite offices that need to scale up or down.

Cape Town office space — FAQs

How much does office space cost in Cape Town?

Indicative prime Grade A asking rents are roughly ZAR 140–230 / m² / month (gross) in sought-after nodes like the CBD/Foreshore, the V&A Waterfront and Century City; serviced offices and coworking are priced per desk. Real pricing depends on grade, fit-out, floor and term — we confirm live figures per option.

Which are the best areas for offices in Cape Town?

The CBD/Foreshore, Century City, Claremont & the Southern Suburbs, the V&A Waterfront, and Bellville/Tyger Valley. The best fit depends on your team's commute, clients and budget.

Is coworking space available in Cape Town?

Yes — a deep coworking and serviced-office market that anchors the "Silicon Cape" tech, BPO and creative scene, concentrated in the CBD, Century City and the Southern Suburbs, with hot desks, dedicated desks and private managed suites on flexible terms.
